# Technical Engineering Levels (I-V)

This framework defines five progressive levels of technical engineers. Each level represents increased responsibility, technical scope, autonomy, and mentorship capacity.

NOTE: The scope of this document specifically does NOT include descriptions of management roles (i.e. their associated duties, expectations, and progression). The current proposal emphasizes a distinction between technical skill and management roles (i.e. consider management as a 'separate axis' of progression).

---

## Level I - Associate Engineer

- **Subtitle:** Entry-Level
- **Experience:** 0-2 years
- **Supervision:** High

### Pre-requisites

- **Required:** None (minimum years of relevant experience)
- **Preferred:** Aerospace or launch-vehicle-oriented internship(s)

### Primary Focus

Learning and execution of well-defined engineering tasks.

### Duties

- Execute assigned design, test, or analysis tasks under close supervision
- Perform data collection, validation, and basic reporting
- Follow established design procedures, documentation, and safety protocols
- Assist in lab work, system assembly, or integration
- Receives day-to-day guidance and technical review from senior engineers
- Participates in design reviews

### Learning & Growth

- Learn industry and company standards, configuration management, and flight hardware lifecycle
- Develop core technical proficiency (e.g., CAD, embedded systems, test automation)
- Begin understanding cross-disciplinary interfaces

### Leadership

- **Role:** Learner
- **Description:** None (learner phase)

### General Goals

- Actively complete and present a team-specific design review
- Late Level-I engineers should be able to mentor interns and new hires

### Management Eligibility

- RE: No
- Lead: No
- Director: No
- Chief: No

## Level II - Engineer

- **Subtitle:**
- **Experience:** 2-4 years
- **Supervision:** Moderate

### Pre-requisites

- **Required:** 2-4 years minimum relevant experience
- **Preferred:** Previous aerospace or launch-vehicle-oriented industry experience

### Primary Focus

Building deep technical capability in a subsystem or discipline. Independently executing technical tasks with occasional guidance.

### Duties

- Design and implement subsystem components or test infrastructure
- Contribute to system integration, environmental testing, and data analysis
- Identify and propose solutions to moderately complex technical issues
- Support documentation, verification, and validation efforts
- Minimum level to become an RE

### Learning & Growth

- Expand technical depth and begin to understand subsystem trade-offs
- Improve design ownership and reliability awareness
- Develop professional engineering judgment and accountability

### Leadership

- **Role:** Peer Support
- **Description:** Mentor Level-I engineers, interns, or new hires; review and check basic work of peers

### General Goals

- Become an owner/RE of a product or process

### Management Eligibility

- RE: Yes
- Lead: No
- Director: No
- Chief: No

---

## Level III - Senior Engineer

- **Subtitle:**
- **Experience:** 4+ years
- **Supervision:** Low

### Pre-requisites

- **Required:** 4+ years minimum relevant experience
- **Preferred:** Previous aerospace or launch-vehicle-oriented industry experience; participation in one or more development, flight, or test campaigns

### Primary Focus

Cradle-to-grave ownership of a subsystem, critical component, or process.

### Duties

- Lead design, analysis, and integration of assigned subsystem(s)
- Perform design reviews, verification plans, and risk analyses
- Coordinate with manufacturing, testing, and mission assurance teams
- Identify long-term improvements for reliability, manufacturability, and performance
- Minimum level to be a lead

### Learning & Growth

- Broaden system-level perspective and cross-disciplinary understanding
- Learn to balance technical rigor with schedule, staffing, and cost constraints
- Develop leadership skills through small project ownership

### Leadership

- **Role:** Mentor
- **Description:** Enforce engineering standards, workflows, and best practices; mentor Levels I-II engineers and provide technical reviews; guide team members through debug, validation, and design-for-flight practices

### General Goals

- Successful delivery of owned subsystem, critical component, or process

### Management Eligibility

- RE: Yes
- Lead: Yes
- Director: No
- Chief: No

---

## Level IV - Staff Engineer

- **Subtitle:**
- **Experience:** 7+ years
- **Supervision:** Minimal

### Pre-requisites

- **Required:** 7+ years minimum relevant experience
- **Preferred:** Previous aerospace or launch-vehicle-oriented industry experience; participation in one or more qualitatively rigorous development, flight, or test campaigns; recognized expert within a discipline or subsystem family

### Primary Focus

Technical leadership across subsystems or programs.

### Duties

- Define and guide technical architecture for key vehicle systems
- Review and approve subsystem designs and test strategies
- Lead cross-functional problem-solving (e.g., failure analysis, root-cause)
- Interface with program management to balance risk, cost, and schedule
- Minimum level to become director

### Learning & Growth

- Develop long-term technical roadmaps and reliability strategies
- Gain experience influencing design direction across departments

### Leadership

- **Role:** Coach
- **Description:** Establish engineering standards, workflows, and best practices; coach senior engineers; provide training and technical guidance across multiple projects

### General Goals

- Define direction of integrated system or processes
- Successful delivery of integrated system or processes

### Management Eligibility

- RE: Yes
- Lead: Yes
- Director: Yes
- Chief: No

---

## Level V - Principal Engineer

- **Subtitle:**
- **Experience:** 12+ years
- **Supervision:** None

### Pre-requisites

- **Required:** 12+ years minimum relevant experience
- **Preferred:** Previous aerospace or launch-vehicle-oriented industry experience; significant contributions to qualitatively rigorous successful flight, development, or test programs

### Primary Focus

Strategic technical direction, system integration, and mentoring at the organizational level.

### Duties

- Serve as the highest individual-contributor authority in key domains
- Can authoritatively re-define established engineering standards and workflows to ensure success
- Define and enforce cross-functional standards, design philosophies, and critical-review criteria
- Lead anomaly investigations and technical review boards
- Advise program directors and Chiefs on risk, performance, and architecture
- Minimum level to be a Chief Engineer

### Learning & Growth

- Maintain cutting-edge expertise and awareness of industry trends
- Drive innovation while preserving mission safety and reliability

### Leadership

- **Role:** Technical Guru
- **Description:** Mentor all lower levels and guide technical leads; shape the engineering culture through example and leadership

### General Goals

- Organizational technical excellence and culture leadership

### Management Eligibility

- RE: Yes
- Lead: Yes
- Director: Yes
- Chief: Yes

## Glossary

- **Responsible Engineer (RE):** Component-level product owner. Defined within the RE policy.
- **Lead:** System-level product owner (e.g. Stage 1 Lead, FTS Lead, Mechanisms Lead, Autopilot Lead)
- **Director:** Department/discipline-level manager (e.g. Avionics Director, Structures Director, GNC Director)
